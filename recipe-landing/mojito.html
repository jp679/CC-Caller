Sugar-Free Mojito

Bright lime, fragrant mint, a kick of white rum, and the gentle fizz of soda water — with zero sugar. Swapping in a few drops of stevia or monk fruit keeps every bit of the sweetness without the crash. Same refreshing mojito, guilt-free.

Prep
5 min
Glass
Highball
Serves
1
Difficulty
Easy
🍹
the recipe

Ingredients & Method

A great sugar-free mojito lives or dies by how you treat the mint. No sugar needed — gentle muddling releases fragrant oils, and a zero-calorie sweetener handles the rest. Respect the leaf.

Ingredients

Serves 1

  • White rum 1½ oz
  • Fresh lime juice 1 oz
  • Liquid stevia or monk fruit sweetener 3–5 drops
  • Fresh mint leaves 6–8
  • Club soda to top
  • Ice crushed

Method

1

Muddle the Mint

Place the mint leaves in the bottom of a sturdy highball glass. Press and twist gently with a muddler — you want to bruise the leaves just enough to release their aromatic oils, not shred them into bits.

Bartender's tip: Use fresh spearmint for the best flavor. Muddle lightly — bruising too hard releases bitter chlorophyll that'll throw off the whole drink.
2

Add the Citrus

Squeeze in the fresh lime juice. The acidity balances the sweetness and lifts the mint. Give it a quick stir to start dissolving the sugar into the lime.

3

Pour the Rum

Add the white rum and 3–5 drops of liquid stevia or monk fruit sweetener. Stir everything together — since there's no sugar to dissolve, it blends instantly. Taste and adjust the sweetness. This is the backbone of the drink — make sure the sweet, sour, and spirit are in harmony before you add the fizz.

4

Ice and Fizz

Fill the glass with crushed ice, then top with club soda. Stir gently — just enough to combine without flattening the carbonation. The drink should look alive with tiny bubbles weaving through the mint.

Bartender's tip: Crushed ice is non-negotiable. It keeps the drink colder longer and dilutes at just the right pace as you sip.
5

Garnish and Serve

Crown it with a fresh sprig of mint — give it a gentle slap between your palms first to wake up the aromatics. Tuck a lime wheel onto the rim. Drink it immediately, preferably somewhere warm with nowhere to be.
