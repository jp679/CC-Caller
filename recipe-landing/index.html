Herb-Crusted Grilled Chicken

Golden char lines, a shatteringly crisp herb crust, and impossibly juicy meat underneath. A proper brine and a screaming-hot grill turn chicken into something you'll crave all week.

Prep
15 min
Cook
25 min
Serves
4
Difficulty
Easy
🍗
the recipe

Ingredients & Method

Great grilled chicken starts before it hits the grate — a quick brine, a punchy herb paste, and the confidence to leave it alone while it cooks.

Ingredients

Serves 4

  • Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs 8 pieces
  • Fresh rosemary, chopped 2 tbsp
  • Fresh thyme leaves 1 tbsp
  • Garlic cloves, minced 4 large
  • Extra virgin olive oil 3 tbsp
  • Lemon, zested and juiced 1 large
  • Smoked paprika 1 tsp
  • Dijon mustard 1 tbsp
  • Kosher salt 1 tbsp
  • Black pepper, freshly cracked generous

Method

1

Make the Herb Paste

Combine the rosemary, thyme, minced garlic, olive oil, lemon zest, smoked paprika, and Dijon mustard in a small bowl. Mix into a thick, fragrant paste. Season with salt and pepper.

Chef's tip: A mortar and pestle gives you a rougher, more rustic paste that adheres better to the skin than a food processor would.
2

Season the Chicken

Pat the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels — this is non-negotiable for crisp skin. Loosen the skin gently and spread half the herb paste directly onto the meat underneath. Rub the remaining paste over the skin. Let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes, or refrigerate uncovered for up to 4 hours.

Chef's tip: Drying the skin and leaving it uncovered in the fridge creates a pellicle — a tacky surface that chars beautifully on the grill.
3

Prepare the Grill

Set up a two-zone fire: one side screaming hot for direct heat, the other side with no coals or burners off for indirect. Clean and oil the grates thoroughly. You want to hear an aggressive sizzle the moment the chicken hits the grill.

4

Grill to Perfection

Place the thighs skin-side down over direct heat. Do not touch them for 5–6 minutes — let the grill do its work until the skin is deeply golden and releases easily. Flip and sear the other side for 2 minutes, then move every piece to the indirect zone. Close the lid and cook for another 12–15 minutes until the internal temperature hits 74°C (165°F).

Chef's tip: Resist the urge to press or move the chicken. Every time you fidget, you lose heat and break the crust. Patience is the secret weapon.
5

Rest and Serve

Transfer the chicken to a cutting board and rest for 5 minutes — the juices need time to redistribute. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the top, scatter with a few torn herb leaves, and serve alongside charred lemon halves. This chicken speaks for itself.
