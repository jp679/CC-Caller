Cumin-Crusted Lamb Chops

A thick, aromatic crust of toasted cumin and smoked paprika seared onto tender lamb — finished with jewel-bright pomegranate seeds, fresh mint, and a drizzle of tahini. Middle Eastern soul, French technique, zero compromise.

Prep
15 min
Cook
12 min
Serves
4
Difficulty
Easy
🥩
the recipe

Ingredients & Method

The secret is a screaming-hot pan, a generous spice crust, and the discipline to pull them at medium-rare. Everything else is just dressing up perfection.

Ingredients

Serves 4

The Lamb

  • Frenched lamb rib chops 8 chops
  • Cumin seeds, toasted and crushed 2 tbsp
  • Coriander seeds, toasted and crushed 1 tbsp
  • Smoked paprika 1 tsp
  • Garlic powder 1 tsp
  • Aleppo pepper flakes 1 tsp
  • Kosher salt generous
  • Black pepper, freshly cracked generous
  • Extra virgin olive oil 3 tbsp

The Finish

  • Pomegranate seeds ½ cup
  • Fresh mint leaves, torn handful
  • Flat-leaf parsley, rough chop handful
  • Tahini 3 tbsp
  • Lemon juice 2 tbsp
  • Cold water 2 tbsp
  • Flaky sea salt (Maldon) for finishing
  • Sumac 1 tsp

Method

1

Build the Spice Crust

Toast the cumin and coriander seeds in a dry skillet over medium heat until fragrant and just starting to pop — about 90 seconds. Crush them coarsely with a mortar and pestle or the bottom of a heavy pan. You want texture, not powder. Mix with the smoked paprika, garlic powder, and Aleppo pepper.

Chef's tip: Pre-ground cumin is a completely different ingredient from freshly toasted and crushed. This step is non-negotiable — it's where 80% of the flavour lives.
2

Prep the Chops

Pat the lamb chops bone-dry with paper towels. Season aggressively with kosher salt and black pepper on both sides. Drizzle with olive oil and press the spice crust firmly onto the meat side of each chop. Let them sit at room temperature for 20 minutes — cold meat in a hot pan is how you get a grey, steamed disaster.

3

Make the Tahini Drizzle

Whisk the tahini with lemon juice and cold water until it loosens into a pourable, creamy consistency. It should flow like heavy cream. Season with a pinch of salt. If it seizes up (tahini does that), add water a teaspoon at a time until it relaxes.

Chef's tip: Always add the lemon juice before the water. Acid first emulsifies the tahini; water first turns it into cement.
4

Sear Hard and Fast

Get a cast-iron skillet ripping hot over high heat — let it preheat for a full 3 minutes. Add a thin film of oil. Lay the chops crust-side down and do not move them for 3 minutes. You want a deep mahogany crust. Flip and sear the other side for 2 minutes. For the fat cap, hold the chops upright with tongs and render the edge for 1 minute.

Chef's tip: Two chops at a time if your pan isn't huge. Overcrowding drops the temperature and you'll steam instead of sear. A proper crust needs space and fury.
5

Rest — This Is Mandatory

Transfer the chops to a wire rack (not a plate — the bottom crust goes soggy on a flat surface) and rest for 5 minutes. The internal temperature will coast up to a perfect 57°C (135°F) for medium-rare. The juices need to redistribute; cut too early and they run out onto the board instead of staying in the meat.

6

Plate Like You Mean It

Arrange the chops on a warm platter, bones pointing up. Drizzle the tahini sauce across the meat in confident, uneven lines. Scatter pomegranate seeds generously — you want jewels of colour in every bite. Tear the mint and parsley over the top, dust with sumac, and finish with flaky sea salt. Serve immediately with warm flatbread and let people eat with their hands.
